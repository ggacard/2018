Books we enjoyed in 2018

A sampler of novels about, or from, Russia and China.

A Gentleman in Moscow

Amor Towles

Count Rostov is condemned to life imprisonment in the Hotel Metropol in Moscow, our favourite hotel. The style, wit, and humour of this novel is unequalled.

The Master and Margarita

Mikhail Bulgakov

The devil and his henchmen visit Moscow and wreak havoc. This satirical masterpiece, written between 1928 and 1940, dissects Muscovite society of the time and mayhem and hilarity ensue.

Fortress Beseiged

Qian Zhongshu

Another satirical masterpiece this time poking fun at Chinese society and manners in the 1930s, much the same period as Master and Margarita in Russia, even if the two could not be more different.

A Hero Born

Jin Yong

The first volume of The Legends of the Condor Heroes, a classic of wuxia, the magical kung fu genre. Our Hero, Guo Jing, comes of age under the tutelage of his devoted, if sometimes bungling, shifu, or teachers
